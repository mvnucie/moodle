Minimum Moodle Expectations

A shared baseline every student can count on - clear access, clear expectations, and consistent course information.

Consistency without uniformity

These expectations do not require every course to look identical. They establish a minimum student-facing structure so students can find what they need, understand what is expected, and track their progress.

1

Syllabus Posted

A current, complete syllabus should be available in Moodle by the first day of class. It should serve as the stable reference point for course expectations, required materials, grading policies, and the course schedule.

2

Clear Course Organization

Course content should follow a predictable weekly, unit, or module structure. Students should be able to identify what they need for the current week without searching through scattered or outdated materials.

3

Assignments and Instructions Listed

All graded work should be represented in Moodle, even if it is completed in class, on paper, or through another platform. Each assignment, quiz, discussion, or graded activity should include clear instructions or a direct link to them.

4

Due Dates Set in Moodle

Due dates should be entered in Moodle activity settings so they appear in the course calendar and student dashboard. Avoid placing due dates only in the syllabus, labels, or text descriptions.

5

Grades Maintained in Moodle

Moodle should serve as the primary student-facing grade record and should be updated regularly throughout the semester. Students need to be able to track their progress before midterm, finals week, or paper grade updates.

6

Materials and Updates Kept Current

Essential course materials and updates should be posted or linked in Moodle with enough lead time for students to plan. When dates, assignments, or expectations change, Moodle should reflect the current reality of the course.

Bottom line

Use Moodle as the reliable home base for course information, expectations, grades, materials, and updates. The CAS template is available as a starting point, but the baseline expectations apply to every course.

CAS Moodle Template
